NHUY CINDY MA
HBAS, MArch

Cindy is an artist, architect, and lifelong learner based in the Waterloo Region.

She is a graduate of the University of Waterloo School of Architecture, where she earned both her HBAS and M.Arch degrees. She has four years of professional experience in architecture and urban design, with co-op positions at HOK, mcCallumSather, Zeidler, and SvN.

Cindy's research and design work explores agency can be expanded within suburban environments. Grounded in urbanism, her work explores where regulations, guidelines, and individual interpretations intersect. She explores methods of representation and abstraction that enable controlled agency and foster social complexity, with the goal of generating vibrant, heterogeneous neighborhoods where moments of unexpected interaction and exploration can thrive.

2402 Urban Canopies Analysis

This project explores laneways, alleys, and other underutilized throughways. In addition to being an underutilized public asset, these spaces take up a significant amount of space within cities. Using the market typology, the intervention proposed for this project combines the use of market stalls and decorative canopies as a means to reactivate these spaces providing users with a more comfortable and aesthetically pleasing environment. Thus, a Grasshopper script is constructed to respond to varying site specific conditions considering the width and height of the laneway as well as the amount of solar radiation received in the summer.

Collaborator: Mahara Falif

Course: Urban Analysis and Simulation
Course Instructor: Cameron Parkin, University of Waterloo
